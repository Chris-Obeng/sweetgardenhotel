Load both through `next/font/google` in `app/layout.tsx` with `display: swap` and inject as CSS variables on the `<html>` element.

## LIQUID BUTTON COMPONENT

This is the signature interactive element of the entire site. Every clickable button must use this component — no plain Tailwind-styled buttons.

**Behaviour:** On hover, a liquid-like organic blob shape emerges from the bottom of the button and fills it entirely, swapping the button's background and text colour. When the cursor leaves, the liquid drains back upward and exits through the top. On mobile touch: the liquid fills on `touchstart` and clears upward on `touchend`.

**Implementation approach:**
- The button contains a hidden `<span>` absolutely positioned to fill the button, with `overflow: hidden` on the parent
- Use GSAP to animate a `clip-path` property on the fill span through three keyframes:
  1. Start: `ellipse(65% 18% at 50% 110%)` — a flat bulge just below the visible area
  2. Mid: `ellipse(80% 45% at 50% 95%)` — rising liquid bubble
  3. End: `ellipse(140% 140% at 50% 50%)` — fully fills the button
- On mouse-leave: reverse through the keyframes, exiting via the top (`ellipse(65% 18% at 50% -10%)`)
- Duration: enter ~0.5s, exit ~0.4s — feels weighty and organic, not snappy
- The text span sits above the fill span (z-index) and its colour transitions to match the new inverted state
- `willChange: 'clip-path'` on the fill element for GPU compositing
- Kill any running timeline before starting a new one (handles fast hover in/out)

**Variants:**
- `outline-dark` — transparent bg, ink border and text. Liquid fill: ink. Text becomes cream on fill.
- `outline-light` — transparent bg, white border and text. Liquid fill: white. Text becomes forest on fill.
- `dark` — forest bg, white text. Liquid fill: white. Text becomes forest on fill.
- `light` — cream bg, ink text. Liquid fill: forest. Text becomes white on fill.

The button is always: `inline-flex items-center justify-center`, `px-8 py-3.5`, `min-w-[160px]`, `text-[11px] tracking-[0.18em] uppercase font-sans`, `overflow-hidden relative`, `cursor-pointer`, `select-none`. No `border-radius` ever.

## ANIMATED TEXT COMPONENT

A reusable component that wraps text and animates it into view on scroll (or on mount). Splits the text into words (or chars for headings) and staggers them in with a translate-Y-up + opacity reveal, exactly as seen on hba.com.

**Approach:** Manually split the text content into spans wrapped in overflow-hidden containers. On ScrollTrigger fire (start: `top 85%`), GSAP animates the inner spans from `y: '100%', opacity: 0` to `y: '0%', opacity: 1` with `stagger: 0.06` (words) or `stagger: 0.03` (chars), `duration: 1`, `ease: 'power4.out'`. Always `once: true` on the ScrollTrigger.

---

## PARALLAX IMAGE COMPONENT

A wrapper component for `next/image` that adds a vertical parallax offset as the user scrolls. Uses GSAP ScrollTrigger with `scrub: true` to translate the inner image element by a configurable amount (default: 20% of its height) from top to bottom of the viewport pass. The outer container must have `overflow: hidden`. The inner image element is scaled/sized to be taller than the container by the parallax amount to prevent gaps.

## CRITICAL NON-NEGOTIABLE DESIGN RULES

1. **NO BORDER RADIUS ON IMAGES — EVER.** Global CSS enforces this. Zero exceptions.

2. **LIQUID BUTTON ONLY.** Every call-to-action button uses `<LiquidButton>`. No plain `<button>` with background colors.

3. **STRICT ALTERNATING SECTION BACKGROUNDS.**
   - Hero: forest (dark)
   - Welcome: cream (light)
   - Rooms: forest (dark)
   - Pool & Garden: forest-mid (dark)
   - Dining: cream (light)
   - Events: forest (dark)
   - Facilities: cream (light)
   - Location: split forest/cream, or full dark
   - Testimonials: cream (light)
   - Footer: forest (dark)

4. **`'use client'` on every interactive component.** GSAP, hooks, browser APIs, and event listeners all require the client directive.

5. **Never use `scroll-behavior: smooth` in CSS.** Lenis owns all scrolling.

6. **No generic placeholder icons.** Use specific Lucide icons semantically matched to each facility (Waves for pool, Leaf for spa, Dumbbell for gym, etc.)

7. **All typography via CSS variables.** `font-family: var(--font-cormorant)` and `var(--font-dm-sans)` — never Tailwind's default font stack for these.

8. **Pool section must be the most dramatic.** Of all sections, `PoolGardenSection` should receive the most visual attention — biggest image, most generous padding, most considered typography.

9. **Affordability is a brand feature.** The Location section and hero metadata should both subtly communicate premium quality at accessible pricing. This is a real selling point for this hotel.

10. **Ghana and Kumasi are features, not afterthoughts.** The eyebrow tag on the hero (`DANYAME · KUMASI · GHANA`) and references to local landmarks in the Location section ground this as a proudly Ghanaian property, not a generic "anywhere" luxury hotel.
